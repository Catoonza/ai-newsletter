# 🤖 AI Weekly — Week of 2026-03-22

*Your dual-layer briefing on everything happening in AI this week.*

## This Week at a Glance

The week's dominant story was a collision between AI governance and geopolitics: Anthropic won a court injunction against the Pentagon's attempt to blacklist it as a supply chain risk, while Congress remained paralysed on federal AI regulation — leaving states, and a pair of progressive senators, to fill the vacuum. Meanwhile, two quiet but significant product signals emerged: OpenAI killed its Sora video app and pivoted toward robotics, and Apple moved toward a multi-model Siri architecture, suggesting the frontier model market is fragmenting faster than anyone expected.

## 🚀 Model Releases & Provider Updates

### The Quick Take

- **Google released [Gemini 3.1 Flash Live](https://deepmind.google/blog/gemini-3-1-flash-live-making-audio-ai-more-natural-and-reliable/)**, a voice-specific model update focused on lower latency and more natural conversation; voice interaction remains the hardest UX problem in AI, and incremental latency gains matter enormously for real-world adoption.
- **Google also launched [Lyria 3 Pro](https://deepmind.google/blog/lyria-3-pro-create-longer-tracks-in-more/)**, its AI music generation model upgrade that generates longer tracks with structural awareness — meaning it can plan a song's arc rather than just string together plausible-sounding bars.
- **Suno released [v5.5](https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/903056/suno-ai-music-v5-5-model)** of its AI music model, emphasising user control with Voices, My Taste, and Custom Models features — a deliberate shift from "impressive demo" toward "personalised creative tool."
- **Amazon Bedrock [expanded to New Zealand](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/machine-learning/run-generative-ai-inference-with-amazon-bedrock-in-asia-pacific-new-zealand/)**, making Claude and Amazon Nova models available in-region — significant for data sovereignty requirements in regulated industries across Asia Pacific.
- **Amazon Polly launched a [Bidirectional Streaming API](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/machine-learning/introducing-amazon-polly-bidirectional-streaming-real-time-speech-synthesis-for-conversational-ai/)**, allowing text-to-speech synthesis to begin before the full text is generated — the infrastructure equivalent of letting a narrator start reading a sentence before the author has finished writing it.
- **Cursor published a [technical report on Composer 2](https://cursor.com/blog/composer-2-technical-report)** alongside a post on [real-time reinforcement learning for Composer](https://cursor.com/blog/real-time-rl-for-composer)**, revealing they are training code-editing AI with live RL feedback — a meaningful departure from static fine-tuning.

## 📚 Research Highlights (arXiv)

### The Quick Take

- **[TurboQuant](https://research.google/blog/turboquant-redefining-ai-efficiency-with-extreme-compression/)** (Google Research): A new compression technique that aggressively reduces model size without major quality loss. Think of it as squeezing a textbook into a pamphlet while retaining most of the information — critical for running capable AI on cheap hardware.
- **[AsgardBench](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/blog/asgardbench-a-benchmark-for-visually-grounded-interactive-planning/)** (Microsoft Research): A new benchmark for embodied AI — testing whether a robot-style agent can look at a scene, plan multi-step actions, and adapt when things go wrong. Current AI still fails badly at this; the benchmark makes those failures measurable.
- **[GroundedPlanBench](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/blog/groundedplanbench-spatially-grounded-long-horizon-task-planning-for-robot-manipulation/)** (Microsoft Research): A companion benchmark specifically for robot manipulation, testing whether vision-language models can generate plans that are spatially precise enough to execute — not just linguistically correct.
- **[LLM Neuroanatomy II / RYS](https://dnhkng.github.io/posts/rys-ii/)**: A researcher found that duplicating a block of seven middle layers in Qwen2-72B — with no additional training — produced a top-ranked model on the HuggingFace leaderboard. The follow-up explores whether this points to a "universal language" of concepts shared across model architectures. If true, it would be a major finding in interpretability.
- **[Vibe Physics](https://www.anthropic.com/research/vibe-physics)** (Anthropic): Anthropic tested Claude as a scientific computing assistant for physics research, finding meaningful productivity gains. This is notable because it extends the "vibe coding" framing — informal, iterative AI collaboration — into hard sciences, not just software.
- **Anthropic's [Economic Index: Learning Curves](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-march-2026-report)**: Anthropic's own economic research division published new data on how workers are learning to use AI tools over time. The "learning curve" framing suggests adoption isn't binary — it compounds, which has implications for how we model AI's economic impact.

## 🏢 AI in Business & Industry

### The Quick Take

- **The Pentagon [formally designated Palantir's Maven Smart System](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/pentagon-formalizes-palantirs-maven-ai-as-a-core-military-system-with-multi-year-funding-platforms-investment-grows-to-usd13-billion-from-usd480-million-in-2024) as an official program of record**, with investment growing from $480M in 2024 to $13B — one of the largest AI military contracts ever formalised, cementing AI-assisted targeting as a permanent fixture of US military infrastructure.
- **A federal judge [blocked the Pentagon's designation of Anthropic as a supply-chain risk](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/902149/anthropic-dod-pentagon-lawsuit-supply-chain-risk-injunction)**, with [Al Jazeera reporting](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/3/25/anthropics-case-against-the-pentagon-could-open-space-for-ai-regulation) the judge suggested the DoD may have been retaliating against Anthropic for its stance on AI weapons restrictions — a rare case of a lab's safety policy directly triggering regulatory conflict.
- **OpenAI [scrapped the Sora app](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/902368/openai-sora-dead-ai-video-generation-competition)**, wound down a $1B Disney deal, and signalled a pivot toward robotics — a significant strategic retreat from consumer video generation at a moment when competition (Runway, Kling, Sora competitors) is intensifying.
- **OpenAI [launched an agentic shopping experience in ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/powering-product-discovery-in-chatgpt)** via the Agentic Commerce Protocol, enabling product discovery and merchant integration — a direct move into territory occupied by Google Shopping and Amazon.
- **The UK's [Financial Conduct Authority gave Palantir a three-month trial](https://www.techradar.com/pro/very-significant-privacy-concerns-uk-hands-over-financial-regulation-data-to-palantir-to-help-tackle-financial-crime)** on its regulatory data, raising "very significant privacy concerns" from critics — illustrating the tension between using AI for public benefit and handing sensitive data to private firms.
- **[David Sacks departed](https://www.theverge.com/policy/902140/david-sacks-out-ai-crypto-czar) as the White House AI and Crypto Czar**, removing the primary Silicon Valley advocate inside the Trump administration at a critical moment for federal AI policy.
- **[Arm launched its first in-house AI chip](https://gizmodo.com/arm-lends-a-hand-launches-in-house-ai-chip-with-meta-as-its-first-customer-2000737555)** with Meta as its inaugural customer — a significant escalation for a company that previously only designed chip architectures and licensed them to others.

## 🛠️ AI Tools & Products

### The Quick Take

- **Apple's [iOS 27 will reportedly let users plug any third-party chatbot into Siri](https://www.theverge.com/tech/902048/apple-siri-ai-chatbot-update-ios-27)** — Claude, Gemini, and others — signalling that Apple is positioning Siri as an orchestration layer rather than competing directly as a model provider. This is the "app store moment" for AI assistants.
- **[Google Gemini launched Import Memory and Import Chat History](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/902085/google-gemini-import-memory-chat-history)**, directly mirroring a recent Anthropic feature — memory portability is quietly becoming a competitive battleground, as switching costs depend on how much your AI "knows" about you.
- **Bluesky unveiled [Attie](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/903190/bluesky-attie-ai-custom-feeds)**, an AI assistant powered by Claude that lets users build custom feed algorithms on the AT Protocol — a decentralised alternative to opaque platform-controlled recommendation systems.
- **OpenAI launched a [Safety Bug Bounty program](https://openai.com/index/safety-bug-bounty)** covering agentic vulnerabilities, prompt injection, and data exfiltration — reflecting that as AI agents gain real-world permissions, adversarial security becomes as important as model quality.
- **Anthropic published details on [Claude Code Auto Mode](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-auto-mode)**, which uses classifiers to automatically approve 93% of low-risk permission prompts — a practical engineering solution to "approval fatigue" in agentic workflows.
- **IBM described [a real-time dashboard for monitoring AI agents](https://www.businessinsider.com/consulting-management-ai-agents-future-ibm-2026-3)** as the future of consulting — with humans supervising agent work rather than doing it. The tooling metaphor is shifting from "assistant" to "workforce."
- **TikTok's [AI ad labelling system is failing](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/900400/tiktok-ai-ads-labels-samsung-disclosure)**, with even expert observers unable to reliably identify AI-generated ads that should be disclosed — a transparency gap with significant consumer protection implications.

## 🐦 From the AI Community

### Notable Discussions This Week

- **[@techdirt](https://techdirt.com) on [Bernie Sanders' Claude interview](https://www.techdirt.com/2026/03/23/bernie-sanders-interviewed-a-chatbot-to-expose-ais-secrets-it-has-no-secrets-it-just-agrees-with-you/)**: Techdirt skewered Sanders' viral video "exposing" AI dangers by pointing out that Claude simply agreed with every leading question — demonstrating sycophancy, not sinister capability. The piece is a useful corrective to both AI panic and AI credulity: the actual problem isn't that the model has secrets, it's that it's designed to agree with you.

- **[Ed Zitron / Where's Your Ed At](https://www.wheresyoured.at/the-ai-industry-is-lying-to-you/)** on AI industry hype: Zitron's widely-shared piece argues that AI companies systematically misrepresent capabilities and economic impacts to sustain investment narratives. Whether you agree or not, it reflects a growing segment of informed observers questioning the gap between benchmark performance and real-world utility.

- **[Luc Julia in Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00882-5)** — the AI researcher and co-creator of Siri called current AI models "glorified pocket calculators," arguing the intelligence framing is fundamentally misleading. Coming from an industry insider, this pushback against capability claims is worth tracking as a signal of internal dissent.

- **[Bruce Schneier](https://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2026/03/as-the-us-midterms-approach-ai-is-going-to-emerge-as-a-key-issue-concerning-voters.html)** on AI as a midterm issue: The security technologist argues that Trump's executive order blocking state AI regulation — combined with Congressional inaction — is creating a democratic accountability vacuum that voters will punish at the midterms. He frames AI governance not as a technical question but as a political power struggle.

- **[Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00893-2)** on conference AI abuse detection: A major academic conference rejected hundreds of papers after detecting LLM use in peer review via watermarks. This is the first high-profile case of watermarking being used operationally to enforce academic integrity — and it won't be the last.

## 📌 One to Watch

**The fragmenting model market and the rise of AI orchestration layers.** Three separate stories this week point to the same underlying shift: Apple opening Siri to multiple chatbots, Google and Anthropic competing on memory portability features, and the Adweek piece arguing that [models are becoming commodities while data is the differentiator](https://www.adweek.com/programmatic/your-ai-model-is-a-commodity-but-your-data-is-a-differentiator/). We may be approaching a phase transition where the "which model is best" conversation becomes less important than "which platform aggregates the right models with the right context." If Siri becomes a router for Claude, Gemini, and GPT-4, then Apple — not any of the model labs — captures the user relationship. Watch for Microsoft, Google, and Amazon to respond with their own orchestration plays over the next quarter.
