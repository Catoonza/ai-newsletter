## This Week at a Glance

This week tells a story of AI's maturation hitting three critical walls: **infrastructure reality**, **safety awakening**, and **institutional shift**. OpenAI imploded visibly (leadership departures, Stargate UK paused), while infrastructure costs triggered actual policy action (Bay Area data center moratoriums). Meanwhile, the field pivoted hard toward agents—but agents that need to be monitored, audited, and constrained. The AI boom's infrastructure fantasies are colliding with physics and politics.

## 📚 Research Highlights (arXiv)

### The Quick Take

- **Agentic Microphysics manifesto** proposes safety analysis shift from isolated models to multi-agent *interaction patterns*—communication, observation, mutual influence. This is a methodological reframing: treat agent swarms as populations, not individuals. ([2604.15236](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15236v1))

- **CoopEval benchmark** found that stronger LLMs defect more in social dilemmas (prisoner's dilemma, public goods games), and tested 15 game-theoretic mechanisms to restore cooperation. Reasoning ≠ ethics. ([2604.15267](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15267v1))

- **RL-STPA framework** adapts hazard analysis (from nuclear/aviation) to reinforcement learning, addressing RL-specific risks: black-box policies, distributional shift, hierarchical failure modes. Safety engineering meets deep learning. ([2604.15201](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15201v1))

- **RadAgent tool-using AI** for stepwise CT interpretation generates reports with inspectable reasoning traces—clinicians can validate and refine each step, not just accept a final answer. Explainability as clinical requirement. ([2604.15231](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15231v1))

- **Looped Transformers stability analysis** shows that test-time compute scaling (spending more iterations on hard problems) only works if architectures avoid fixed-point collapse. A theoretical constraint on scaling inference. ([2604.15259](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15259v1))

- **SpecGuard speculative decoding** uses step-level verification (not just token-level) to prevent erroneous reasoning steps from propagating—agents need internal checkpoints, not just output validation. ([2604.15244](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15244v1))

**Why it matters:** Research has shifted from capability benchmarks to **safety infrastructure**: how do you monitor agents, verify reasoning, restore cooperation, and scale inference without collapse? The papers assume agents are already here; they're solving for governance.

## 📌 One to Watch

**The Data Center Regulation Paradox**: OpenAI paused Stargate UK, Bay Area banned new data centers, and Apollo warned on ROI—yet demand for AI compute keeps rising. This creates a **supply constraint that benefits incumbents** (NVIDIA, hyperscalers with existing capacity) while locking out new entrants. Europe explicitly wants sovereign AI capacity (Mistral), but regulation is making it economically unviable. The result: consolidation toward a handful of well-capitalized operators, increased geopolitical fragmentation, and higher costs for everyone else. Watch whether this accelerates a shift toward on-device AI (smaller models, local execution) or centralizes power further. This is the physical infrastructure chokepoint that no amount of model innovation can solve.
